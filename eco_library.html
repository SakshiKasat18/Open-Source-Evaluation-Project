Eco Library: Carbon Emission & Climate Facts

Understand the power of small actions, the danger of inaction, and the beauty of sustainability.

1. Only 100 companies are responsible for more than 70% of global industrial emissions.

2. A single flight from Delhi → London emits ~1.9 tons of CO₂ per passenger.

3. Half of all human emissions ever produced occurred after 1990 — within one generation.

4. Plastic production releases CO₂ at every stage — extraction, manufacturing, and decomposition.

5. One mature tree absorbs ~20 kg CO₂ per year. Forests store 45% of Earth’s land carbon.

6. Food waste causes 8–10% of global emissions — the 3rd largest emitter if it were a country.

7. Beef emits 20–30× more CO₂ than plant-based food. Each kilogram ≈ 70–100 showers’ worth.

8. Cycling produces 30× less CO₂ than driving the same distance.

9. Switching to renewable energy can reduce household emissions by up to 40%.

10. Electric vehicles emit 40–60% less CO₂ over their lifetime than petrol cars.

11. The wealthiest 1% produce more emissions than the poorest 66% combined.

12. Streaming one 4K movie can use as much energy as running a fridge for an hour!

13. Oceans absorb nearly 30% of CO₂ we produce — but rising temperatures reduce their ability to do so.

14. Deforestation accounts for about 10% of global emissions — mostly from agriculture expansion.

15. Recycling aluminum saves 95% of the energy needed to make new aluminum.

16. Using LED bulbs instead of incandescent lights cuts electricity emissions by 80%.
